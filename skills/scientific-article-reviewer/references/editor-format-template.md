# Editor Report Template

Use this template when the user requests the formal editor-facing report (Phase 5).
Fill it based entirely on the internal review already completed — do not re-analyze.
Translate findings into a formal, collegial register appropriate for journal submission.

If the user provides a journal-specific format, discard this template and follow their format exactly.

---

## Template

```
REVIEWER REPORT
Manuscript title: [Full title]
Recommendation: [Accept / Minor Revision / Major Revision / Reject]

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
COMMENTS TO THE EDITOR (CONFIDENTIAL)
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
[Write 2-4 sentences for the editor only. Include:
 - Your overall assessment of the manuscript's scientific merit
 - The main reason behind your recommendation
 - If Critical: flag any integrity concerns (unverifiable citations, possible data issues)
   here in the confidential section, not in the author comments
 - Do NOT include harsh personal judgments — be professional]

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
COMMENTS TO THE AUTHORS
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Thank you for submitting your manuscript entitled "[Title]" for review.

[One short paragraph with a balanced general assessment:
acknowledge the topic's relevance and any genuine strengths before moving to concerns]

MAJOR COMMENTS:

1. [State the problem clearly. Explain why it matters scientifically.
   Indicate what would be needed to resolve it — be specific about what
   additional data, analysis, or clarification is required.]

2. [Next major issue...]

[Continue numbered list for all major issues]

MINOR COMMENTS:

1. [Brief, specific comment — location + what needs to change]
2. [...]

[Continue numbered list for all minor issues]

REFERENCES:

The following references require verification by the authors. During my review,
I was unable to confirm the existence or accuracy of these citations:

[For each unverified/suspicious reference:]
- [Authors, Year, Title, Journal] — [Brief note: "could not be located",
  "title does not match the cited claim", etc.]

Please ensure all references are accurate and accessible.
```

---

## Notes on tone and register

- **Major comments** should be clearly problems, but framed as opportunities to strengthen the work: "The authors should provide..." / "It is unclear how..." / "The analysis would benefit from..."
- **Minor comments** are typically phrased as direct suggestions: "Line 34: replace X with Y" / "Figure 2 caption should define the error bars"
- Avoid accusatory language, especially around citations — phrase as requests for verification rather than accusations of fraud
- For **Critical** recommendations: the recommendation to the editor becomes "Reject" in the template, and the integrity concerns go in the confidential Editor section only
- Match the language to what the user requested (Spanish or English)
