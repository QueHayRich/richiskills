# Comprehensive Reference List: Molecular Modeling, Docking, and Molecular Dynamics

Compiled 2026-03-18. All entries verified via web search.

---

## 1. General Computational Chemistry / Molecular Modeling Textbooks

### Core Textbooks

1. **Molecular Modelling: Principles and Applications**
   - Author: Andrew R. Leach
   - Publisher: Pearson Education (Prentice Hall)
   - Edition: 2nd Edition, 2001
   - ISBN: 978-0582382107
   - Notes: Widely regarded as the definitive molecular modeling textbook. Covers quantum mechanics, molecular mechanics, energy minimization, MD, Monte Carlo, conformational analysis, free energies, drug design, and protein structure.

2. **Essentials of Computational Chemistry: Theories and Models**
   - Author: Christopher J. Cramer
   - Publisher: Wiley
   - Edition: 2nd Edition, 2004
   - ISBN: 978-0470091821
   - Notes: Excellent graduate-level text covering classical models through quantum mechanical and dynamical theories.

3. **Introduction to Computational Chemistry**
   - Author: Frank Jensen
   - Publisher: Wiley
   - Edition: 3rd Edition, 2017
   - ISBN: 978-1118825990
   - Notes: Comprehensive account of fundamental principles. 3rd ed. adds polarizable force fields, tight-binding DFT, accelerated MD methods, tensor decomposition, and more extensive DFT functionals.

4. **Computational Chemistry: Introduction to the Theory and Applications of Molecular and Quantum Mechanics**
   - Author: Errol G. Lewars
   - Publisher: Springer
   - Edition: 3rd Edition, 2016
   - ISBN: 978-3-319-30914-9
   - Notes: Accessible overview for beginners with comparisons of methods.

5. **Computational Chemistry and Molecular Modeling: Principles and Applications**
   - Authors: K. I. Ramachandran, Gopakumar Deepa, Krishnan Namboori
   - Publisher: Springer
   - Edition: 2008
   - ISBN: 978-3-540-77304-7
   - Notes: Graduate/postgraduate textbook with in-depth theoretical background and applications in chemistry, life sciences, and engineering.

6. **Molecular Modelling for Beginners**
   - Author: Alan Hinchliffe
   - Publisher: Wiley
   - Edition: 2nd Edition, 2008
   - ISBN: 978-0470513149
   - Notes: Concise basic introduction focusing on MM, MC, MD, QSAR, and discovery chemistry with hands-on examples.

### Molecular Simulation Classics

7. **Understanding Molecular Simulation: From Algorithms to Applications**
   - Authors: Daan Frenkel, Berend Smit
   - Publisher: Academic Press / Elsevier
   - Edition: 3rd Edition, 2023 (2nd ed. 2002; 1st ed. 1996)
   - ISBN (3rd ed.): 978-0323902922
   - ISBN (2nd ed.): 978-0122673511
   - Notes: THE classic reference for molecular simulation algorithms. Explains MC, MD, free energy methods, and advanced sampling from a statistical mechanics perspective.

8. **Computer Simulation of Liquids**
   - Authors: Michael P. Allen, Dominic J. Tildesley
   - Publisher: Oxford University Press
   - Edition: 2nd Edition, 2017 (1st ed. 1987)
   - ISBN (2nd ed.): 978-0198803195
   - ISBN (1st ed.): 978-0198556459
   - Notes: Foundational reference for MD and MC simulation techniques. The 2017 edition includes accompanying programs in Fortran and Python.

9. **Statistical Mechanics**
   - Author: Donald A. McQuarrie
   - Publisher: University Science Books
   - Edition: 2000
   - ISBN: 978-1891389153
   - Notes: Classic statistical mechanics textbook providing essential theoretical foundations for molecular simulation.

## 2. Molecular Docking and Structure-Based Drug Design Books

10. **Molecular Docking for Computer-Aided Drug Design: Fundamentals, Techniques, Resources and Applications**
    - Author: M. Coumar
    - Publisher: Academic Press / Elsevier
    - Edition: 1st Edition, 2021
    - ISBN: 978-0128223123
    - Notes: Comprehensive coverage of SBDD methodologies in three sections: basic techniques, tools/web servers, and applications.

11. **Drug Design: Structure- and Ligand-Based Approaches**
    - Editors: Kenneth M. Merz Jr., Dagmar Ringe, Charles H. Reynolds
    - Publisher: Cambridge University Press
    - Edition: 2010
    - ISBN: 978-0521887236
    - Notes: Covers X-ray crystallography, NMR, fragment-based drug design, free energy methods, docking/scoring, QSAR, pharmacophore methods, computational ADME-Tox.

12. **The Practice of Medicinal Chemistry**
    - Editor: Camille Georges Wermuth (4th ed. with David Aldous, Pierre Raboisson, Didier Rognan)
    - Publisher: Academic Press / Elsevier
    - Edition: 4th Edition, 2015
    - ISBN: 978-0124172050
    - Notes: Known as "the Bible" of medicinal chemistry. Covers screening, scoring, docking, binding free energy calculations, polypharmacology, QSAR, and drug discovery methodology.

13. **An Introduction to Medicinal Chemistry**
    - Author: Graham L. Patrick
    - Publisher: Oxford University Press
    - Edition: 7th Edition, 2023
    - ISBN: 978-0198866664
    - Notes: Complete and accessible approach to medicinal chemistry including computational methods chapter.

14. **Molecular Modeling: Basic Principles and Applications**
    - Authors: Hans-Dieter Holtje, Wolfgang Sippl, Didier Rognan, Gerd Folkers
    - Publisher: Wiley-VCH (Methods and Principles in Medicinal Chemistry series)
    - Edition: 3rd Edition, 2008
    - ISBN: 978-3527315680
    - Notes: From simple calculations on small molecules to modeling of proteins and biomolecules.

15. **Computational Drug Discovery and Design**
    - Editor: Riccardo Baron
    - Publisher: Humana Press / Springer (Methods in Molecular Biology, Vol. 819)
    - Edition: 2nd Edition, 2023 (1st ed. 2012)
    - ISBN (2nd ed.): 978-1-0716-3440-0
    - ISBN (1st ed.): 978-1617794643
    - Notes: Practical protocols for drug target identification, virtual screening, lead optimization, and pharmacokinetic prediction.

## 3. Molecular Dynamics Simulation Books

16. **Molecular Dynamics Simulation: Elementary Methods**
    - Author: J. M. Haile
    - Publisher: Wiley
    - Edition: 1992
    - ISBN: 978-0471184393
    - Notes: Classic introduction demonstrating how MD simulations work and how to perform them.

17. **Molecular Simulations: Fundamentals and Practice**
    - Authors: Saman Alavi
    - Publisher: Wiley-VCH
    - Edition: 2020
    - ISBN: 978-3527341054
    - Notes: Covers Newtonian mechanics, force-field methods, Monte Carlo, MD in detail, plus advanced techniques and practical setup advice.

18. **Molecular Dynamics: With Deterministic and Stochastic Numerical Methods**
    - Authors: Ben Leimkuhler, Charles Matthews
    - Publisher: Springer (Interdisciplinary Applied Mathematics, Vol. 39)
    - Edition: 2015
    - ISBN: 978-3-319-16375-8
    - Notes: Mathematical underpinnings of MD algorithms: symplectic methods, constraints, Langevin dynamics, thermostats, barostats, multiple time-stepping.

19. **Molecular Dynamics Simulations in Statistical Physics: Theory and Applications**
    - Author: Giovanni Ciccotti (and others)
    - Publisher: Springer
    - Edition: 2020
    - ISBN: 978-3-030-35702-3
    - Notes: Recipes for MD simulations in statistical physics with focus on macromolecular systems.

20. **Numerical Simulation in Molecular Dynamics: Numerics, Algorithms, Parallelization, Applications**
    - Authors: Michael Griebel, Stephan Knapek, Gerhard Zumbusch
    - Publisher: Springer
    - Edition: 2007
    - ISBN: 978-3-540-68095-6
    - Notes: Detailed treatment of linked-cell method, SPME method, tree codes, multipole technique, and MPI parallel implementation.

21. **Biomolecular Simulations: Methods and Protocols**
    - Editors: Luca Monticelli, Emppu Salonen
    - Publisher: Humana Press / Springer (Methods in Molecular Biology, Vol. 924)
    - Edition: 2013
    - ISBN: 978-1627030168
    - Notes: Practical protocols for electronic structure calculations, classical MD, and coarse-grained techniques applied to proteins, lipids, nucleic acids, and carbohydrates.

22. **Biomolecular Simulations: Methods and Protocols** (2nd volume)
    - Editors: Massimiliano Bonomi, Carlo Camilloni
    - Publisher: Humana Press / Springer (Methods in Molecular Biology, Vol. 2022)
    - Edition: 2019
    - Notes: Updated protocols and methods for biomolecular simulation.

## 4. Free Energy Calculations Books

23. **Free Energy Calculations: Theory and Applications in Chemistry and Biology**
    - Editors: Christophe Chipot, Andrew Pohorille
    - Publisher: Springer (Springer Series in Chemical Physics, Vol. 86)
    - Edition: 2007
    - ISBN: 978-3540736172
    - Notes: Comprehensive account of free energy concepts: formally accurate and approximate methods, classical and quantum mechanical descriptions, applied to conformational equilibria, partitioning, receptor-drug interactions, and protein associations.

24. **Free Energy Computations: A Mathematical Perspective**
    - Authors: Tony Lelievre, Mathias Rousset, Gabriel Stoltz
    - Publisher: Imperial College Press / World Scientific
    - Edition: 2010
    - ISBN: 978-1848162471
    - Notes: Rigorous mathematical treatment of free energy calculation methods for practitioners and applied mathematicians.

## 5. Protein Structure and Structural Bioinformatics Books

25. **Protein Structure and Function**
    - Authors: Gregory A. Petsko, Dagmar Ringe
    - Publisher: Sinauer Associates / Oxford University Press (Primers in Biology series)
    - Edition: 2004
    - ISBN: 978-0878936632 / 978-0199556847
    - Notes: Accessible introduction to protein structure, folding, functional properties, and regulation.

26. **Structural Bioinformatics**
    - Editors: Jenny Gu, Philip E. Bourne
    - Publisher: Wiley
    - Edition: 2nd Edition, 2009
    - ISBN: 978-0470181058
    - Notes: Application of bioinformatics principles to macromolecular structure; prediction of protein structure and function. Widely used as a graduate textbook.

27. **Introduction to Protein Structure Prediction: Methods and Algorithms**
    - Editors: Huzefa Rangwala, George Karypis
    - Publisher: Wiley (Wiley Series in Bioinformatics)
    - Edition: 2010
    - ISBN: 978-0470470596
    - Notes: Covers background on structure prediction, prediction of structural elements, tertiary structure prediction, and functional insights.

28. **From Protein Structure to Function with Bioinformatics**
    - Editors: Daniel J. Rigden
    - Publisher: Springer
    - Edition: 2nd Edition, 2017
    - ISBN: 978-94-024-1069-3
    - Notes: Structure-based methods for function assignment based on folds, surface characteristics, 3D motifs, protein-protein interactions, and dynamic properties.

29. **Introduction to Bioinformatics**
    - Author: Arthur M. Lesk
    - Publisher: Oxford University Press
    - Edition: 5th Edition, 2019
    - ISBN: 978-0198794141
    - Notes: Comprehensive bioinformatics textbook with chapters on structural bioinformatics and drug discovery.

## 6. Force Fields and Parametrization Books

30. **Chemoinformatics: Basic Concepts and Methods**
    - Editors: Thomas Engel, Johann Gasteiger
    - Publisher: Wiley-VCH
    - Edition: 2018
    - ISBN: 978-3-527-33109-3
    - Notes: Covers representation of molecular structures, data types, databases, search methods, data analysis, plus applications in structure elucidation, reaction simulation, synthesis planning, and drug design. Force field concepts covered extensively.

56. **Bussi, G., Donadio, D., & Parrinello, M.** (2007). Canonical Sampling through Velocity Rescaling. *J. Chem. Phys.*, 126, 014101.
